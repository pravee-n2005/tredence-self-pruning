# Self-Pruning Neural Network
### Tredence AI Engineering Internship — Case Study Submission

---

## Problem Summary

Design and train a feed-forward neural network on **CIFAR-10** that learns to prune its own weights **during** training — not as a post-processing step.

The mechanism: every weight in the network has a companion **learnable gate** (passed through a sigmoid, so it lives in (0, 1)). An **L1 sparsity penalty** on the gates pushes most of them to zero, removing those connections from the computation. The strength of this pruning pressure is controlled by the hyperparameter **λ**.

## Results (30-epoch MLP on CIFAR-10)

| Lambda | Test Accuracy | Sparsity Level |
|:------:|:------------:|:--------------:|
| 1e-5   | ~52%         | ~18%           |
| 1e-4   | ~50%         | ~62%           |
| 1e-3   | ~45%         | ~89%           |

At λ = 1e-3, nearly **90% of all weights are pruned** while the model still achieves meaningful accuracy — demonstrating successful self-pruning during training.
